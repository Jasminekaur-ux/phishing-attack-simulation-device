How to Spot Phishing Emails

  • Urgent or threatening language: Attackers often use fear or urgency to make you act quickly without thinking.
  • Suspicious sender address: Check if the email address matches the sender name and organization.
  • Unexpected attachments or links: Be wary of unsolicited files or links, especially if they ask for login details.
  • Poor spelling and grammar: Legitimate organizations usually have professional communication.
  • Requests for personal info: Reputable companies rarely ask for passwords or sensitive data via email.
